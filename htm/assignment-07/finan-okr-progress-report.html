✅ 3. Progress

What the Organization Needed

Filipino Nurses Association-Nordic (FiNAN) is a registered non-profit organization dedicated to connecting, supporting, and advocating for Filipino nurses across the Nordic region.

FiNAN serves as a professional bridge for Filipino nurses working in: 🇩🇰 Denmark, 🇫🇴 Faroe Islands, 🇫🇮 Finland (including 🇦🇽 Åland), 🇬🇱 Greenland, 🇮🇸 Iceland, 🇳🇴 Norway, 🇸🇪 Sweden

Official Registrations: Finland (PRH: 3084026-2), Denmark (Erhvervsstyrelsen: 40349367), Sweden (Skatteverket: 802537-7097), Iceland (Ríkisskattstjóri: 5208190450)

Mission Focus:

  • Licensing & Accreditation: Navigating regulatory requirements in each Nordic country
  • Cultural Integration: Helping nurses thrive in new social and professional environments
  • Professional Development: Promoting continuous learning and career growth
The problem: The membership process was fully manual. Members registered through the website, then paid via bank transfer. A board member had to manually verify each payment before granting access to member content. This workflow was slow, error-prone, difficult to scale, and depended entirely on specific individuals. There was no technical documentation for handover or onboarding.

What I Delivered

  • ✅ Redesigned website — deployed finan.eu.com with improved performance and maintainability
  • ✅ Built Stripe automation — automated membership registration and payment flow, eliminated manual bank-transfer verification
  • ✅ Customized Ghost CMS theme — modified Ghost theme to meet FiNAN's specific needs, source code available at github.com/finan-eu/finan-puls
  • ✅ Migrated email infrastructure — moved from legacy setup to FastMail, improved security and communication reliability
  • ✅ Created technical documentation — comprehensive docs at docs.finan.eu.com for onboarding and future handover

Stakeholder Dynamics

Some board members preferred keeping manual checks (felt safer). Resolved by mapping full process with them, showing where manual steps created delays and avoidable errors. Agreed on phased automation with clear checkpoints and visibility.

Current Blockers

  • ⏳ Waiting on board approval — need FiNAN board sign-off before publishing case study publicly

💡 5. Learnings & Insights

✅ What Worked Well

Real production work > tutorial projects — FiNAN is actual impact with measurable outcomes

Documentation while building — writing knowledge base during implementation made slides easier

Phased approach wins buy-in — incremental rollout reduced stakeholder resistance

⚠️ Challenges Faced

Stakeholder alignment takes time — should have started approval conversation earlier

Metrics not tracked upfront — didn't measure baseline admin time before automation

Scope ambiguity — clarified deliverables before committing to avoid misunderstandings

🔍 Unexpected Observations

Volunteer work resonates stronger — non-profit with social impact is more compelling

Technical depth needs tuning — need separate executive summary for non-technical recruiters

Influence without authority — earned alignment through clarity and reliable execution

🔁 Patterns to Repeat

Map the full workflow with stakeholders before proposing solutions

Bring options with risks explained in business terms

Document as you go — future maintainers will thank you

Start approval conversations early with external organizations
